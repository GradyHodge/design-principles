Web Design Principles

Recognize and identify favorable design principles in existing sites.

Hick's Law

Designing Long Menu Lists Well Hick's Law (or the Hick–Hyman Law) says that the more choices you present to your users, the longer it takes them to reach a decision. However, combining Hick’s Law with other design techniques can make long menus easy to use.

Relevance

The Porche website combines several menus and decisions to make based on other lists without the user feeling overwhelmed with their choices. The use of images, icons, slide bars, and white space as part of the menu selection prevents the user from becoming overwhelmed and opens up the process of customizing the users product.

Rule of 3rd's

How to Use it and when to break it
Basically, you draw a tic-tac-toe style grid over your image or layout. Your points of interest should fall on these lines or where the lines intersect.

Relevance

In this website, skillful attention to a simple concept, such as the rule of thirds, will persuade your viewers attention to find the websites "call to action".

Fitts' Law

Fitts's Law describes how long it takes a user to hit a target in a graphical user interface (GUI) or other design, as a function of size and distance. Understanding this law helps us design better buttons, forms, lists, and other interactive elements.
